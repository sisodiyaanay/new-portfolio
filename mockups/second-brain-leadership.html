Gamed-metric guarddesign note

Raw token leaderboards fail the same way lines-of-code leaderboards failed. Amazon and Meta both dismantled raw-output rankings after engineers learned to optimise the number instead of the work — verbose prompting, redundant sessions, context stuffing. Any metric a person can inflate without creating value, they eventually will.

Second Brain therefore never ranks engineers by tokens. What it tracks instead:

Decisions captured per $1k of spend
judgment density, not volume
Session-to-commit rate, normalized by task type
did the motion ship?
Verified outputs per engineer-week
rigor survives efficiency gains

Efficient engineers who spend fewer tokens on the same outcome score better here, not worse.

What each status means Definitions of converge, plateau and abandon

Converge: The engineer reached a verified, usable output within the session — the AI provided meaningful help and the engineer locked in the result. Indicates strong prompt design, healthy verification habits, and a well-calibrated session loop.

Plateau: The engineer made progress but stalled — work continues without a clear closing decision. Typically caused by missing context, an ambiguous goal, or low verification. Often resolvable with a single coaching nudge.

Abandon: The engineer stopped the session without a usable outcome. Healthy in small doses (problem re-framing), unhealthy as a pattern — it points to either a poor task fit for AI or a confidence gap the engineer is not yet able to bridge.
